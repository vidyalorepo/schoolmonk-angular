Those who complete B. Tech in any discipline are designated as engineers. Getting a good job after graduation is a major concern for most students. It is not just about the IT industry which offers great career opportunities to B. Tech graduates but other disciplines also offer good scope for B. Tech degree holders. Studying B. Popular BE/B. Tech industries abroad for Jobs and Career Opportunities

After completing the B. Tech degree, students want to work abroad and have a fruitful career. Below mentioned are some of the popular job roles that students can opt for after completing their B. Tech.

  • Information Technology: Information technology is the most sought-after field in today's time. Computer Science graduates and information technology degree holders are in much demand abroad. Computer Science graduates are in high demand in the US.
  • Mechanical Engineering: With the broadening of scope and technological advancement, the job prospects of mechanical engineers are growing tremendously. As this is an evergreen field, so, the demand for mechanical engineer go beyond geographical boundaries. Also, with increasing automation, mechanical engineering has become one of the most sought after branches of engineering.
  • Petroleum Engineering: One of the main sources of energy that is widely used in the 20th and 21st centuries. It is one of the biggest and most profitable industries in the world. Petroleum engineers are involved in the exploration and production of oil and gas. They are responsible for increasing production and dealing with different technical aspects of drilling.
  • Civil Engineering: Civil Engineering is a promising branch of engineering that offers extensive opportunities to graduates. Initially, remuneration may not be huge but with time and experience, the pay package also increases. Growth in other fields may become stagnant with time, but in civil engineering, experience makes the professional even more valuable.

Academic Requirements

Prominent colleges prefer candidates who have completed their 12th standard with excellent scores from recognized education boards. Other requirements are mentioned below:

  • For studying an undergraduate degree, you must have completed your 12th standard from a recognized education board.
  • Language proficiency exams IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, and standardized exams like SAT or ACT.

Application requirements

Apart from your academic documents and transcripts, there are some other application requirements such as:

  • Resume
  • Reference letters/Letter of recommendations
  • Essays (as demanded by the colleges)

Application process

First of all, start researching about colleges offering BE/B.Tech abroad. If you have decided on the country before selecting the college then you can search about the colleges according to your profile. If you have not decided on the country before selecting the college, you can compare a few colleges on factors like tuition fees, living costs, and eligibility criteria.

Exams for BE/B.Tech Abroad

For getting admission to an engineering college abroad, students need to give English Language proficiency exams like IELTS, TOEFL, and PTE. Also, for getting into an undergraduate course, students also need to give standardized exams like the SAT and ACT. The exams can vary from university to university, so, students should know which exam to give for which course to study abroad.

Scholarships to Study BE/B. Tech Abroad

Pursuing BE/B. Tech can be costly for some students, however, there are various scholarships that students can apply to and continue with their plans of studying engineering abroad.

Global Study Awards

This scholarship is applicable for both bachelor's and master’s degrees and is applicable to the USA, Canada, Australia, UK, and Singapore. Global Study Awards scholarship can be used for tuition and living expenses.

Merit Scholarship for UG students

The ECPI University awards a limited number of merit scholarships to graduates enrolling in selected programs every year. The Merit scholarship is applicable to various technology courses offered in a bachelor's program.
