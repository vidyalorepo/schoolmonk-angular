How to Study Abroad after class 12

If you are planning to study abroad, the admission process will require you to give one language exam and one standardised exam. However, depending on the country, course, college or university you are targeting, you may be exempted from submitting standardised exams, but you would still be required to take One language exam to show English language proficiency. Depending on the course and college/university, you may also be required to provide scores on one standardised test. Check out the different exams to study abroad for international students.

Advantages of studying abroad after 12th

  • Studying abroad after the 12th can help you become a global citizen by giving you access to a variety of options in life. Being a global citizen early in your career teaches you how to make decisions on your own and how to adapt to new situations.
  • Having a UG degree from another country makes you more marketable to employers everywhere. The likelihood of receiving a stay-back option after completing your education in the nation is very high.
  • This is the best approach to enhancing your language proficiency and learning about the nation and culture. This makes it easier to continue your future in the same country.
  • Your ability to be independent and recognize your genuine self is enhanced by undergraduate study abroad.
  • The course curriculum at institutions abroad is well-developed, with an emphasis on practical learning rather than academic knowledge.
  • Even though studying abroad is expensive, you end up earning more money, which balances your financial condition in the long run.
  • Studying abroad helps you build your individuality by mastering skills such as time and money management, acclimating to new environments, independence, etc.
  • Additionally, studying abroad after the 12th gives you a chance to learn about things such as a new language, cuisine, culture, people, and surroundings, which expands your knowledge.

Exams to Study Abroad

International students applying to foreign universities will have to take certain exams in order to be be eligible to study in these foreign countries. Read on the types of abroad exams that are mandatory for submission to study abroad.

Language exams

The language exams show your English proficiency skills. You need to give either one of the language exams to get admission to any university in an English-speaking country. These are required for pursuing almost all courses; Undergraduate courses, Postgraduate courses, Certificates or Diplomas (unless mentioned). Different countries give preference to one language exam over others. Few of the top English language exams which are used by prominent study-abroad destinations:

  • IELTS: IELTS exam is the preferred exam for getting admission to universities in the UK, Australia, and New Zealand. IELTS Score is valid for two years.
  • TOEFL: TOEFL exam is the preferred exam for universities in the US and Canada, while it is not accepted at many colleges in the UK. Your TOEFL scores are valid until two years post the declaration of your result.
  • PTE: PTE exam is accepted at a few colleges in the US and most colleges in the UK. CAE and CPE are given preference by British universities. Further, PTE Academic scores are valid for up to two years from the date of the exam.

Standardised Entrance Exams for Foreign Universities

The entrance exams for foreign universities for each course can be different, varying in the area of study or at the level of study as well. The exams required for studying various courses are different. Depending on the courses of your choice, you will be required to give only one standardized entrance exam for abroad education. So, if you are going to pursue MS then you have to give exams for MS abroad. Similarly, if you are going to pursue an MBA abroad so you have to give exams for MBA abroad. Also, there are different countries' standardized tests for different countries.

Exams for Study Abroad with Scholarship
The most popular and important entrance exam to study abroad is GRE, GMAT, IELTS, TOEFL, SAT, and ACT. Excellent performance in these international competitive exams can also get students financial aid in the form of scholarships. Many colleges and universities consider the performance of the aspirant in these international entrance exams for giving scholarships to aspirants at the time of giving admission.

Exams to Study Abroad after 12th
Those students who have cleared their higher secondary have the option to give various entrance exams for studying abroad. SAT, ACT, GRE, TOEFL, and IELTS are some of the important standardised tests which students can give for going to study abroad. The choice of exam always depends on the course, college, and country where you want to go.
