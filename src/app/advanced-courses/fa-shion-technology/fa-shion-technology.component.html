How to be a Fashion Designer: Know what you need to be a successful Fashion Industry

Fashion design involves applying design, aesthetics, clothing construction, and natural beauty to clothing and its accessories. Different trends and cultures impact the way clothing is made, as well as the way it is worn over time. The profession of fashion design requires a variety of skills, from market research to creativity to fabric selection, and to succeed in this profession, designers must be attuned to market trends constantly.

To become a fashion designer after class 12, students must pursue a bachelor's degree in fashion design followed by a master's degree or postgraduate diploma. B. Des fashion design and BA fashion design are the two popular fashion design courses after the 12th. Students need to pass the 10+2 from any stream with a 50% aggregate score to get admission to top fashion design colleges. NIFT, Symbiosis Pune, and Pearl Academy are the top fashion design colleges in India.

Candidates who are willing to pursue Fashion Designing Courses Online can apply directly whereas those who want to get admission in Top Fashion Design Colleges appear for entrance exams like NIFT, IIAD, NID DAT, etc.

Graduates can apply for job roles of textile designer, Fashion Designer, fashion blogger, fashion journalist, costume designer, fashion editor, and many more

Career options after pursuing Fashion Designing courses after 12th-

Fashion Designing opens a world of opportunities if you are creative, innovative and are abreast of the latest fashion market trends. Some of the career options that you can pursue after finishing a course in Fashion Designing after 12th are: Fashion Coordinator, Fashion Designer, Fashion Stylist, Fashion Consultant, Textile Designer, Jewellery and Footwear Designer, Retail Managers, Fashion Buyer, Make-up Artist, Personal Shopper, etc.

Students may choose to pursue whichever of the fashion designing courses after 12th that seems suitable to them.

Fashion Designing Courses After 12th Eligibility

After clearing their 12th grade, candidates can pursue bachelor's and master's degrees in fashion design. To be admitted to these courses, students must get a minimum score of 50% in 10+2. Admission standards will be entirely merit-based, as determined by a written test, a group discussion, and an interview.

Type Of Course Eligibility Criteria
UG Courses The candidates must gain at least 50% aggregate marks, in their 12th examination, for pursuing Fashion Designing courses at the bachelor’s level. Candidates performing the above eligibility, are likely to appear for the entrance exams like NIFT, NID DAT, and UCEED
PG courses Candidates seeking fashion designing courses at the master’s level, need to hold a bachelor’s degree in the relevant stream, with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks from a recognized institution. Candidates performing the above eligibility, are likely to appear for the entrance exams like CEED, NID DAT, IIAD, etc.
Diploma Courses Candidates must come through their 10+2 exams from a recognized institute with aggregate marks, ranging between 40 to 50%.

Fashion Design Courses After 12th Admission Process

Depending on the degree an applicant chooses admission to fashion designing courses after 12th may vary.

  • Candidates interested in fashion design certificate programs will be admitted directly. Aspirants can go to the official website of the college they want to attend and select a specific certificate programme in fashion designing courses after 12th.
  • Candidates for fashion designing courses after 12th diplomas and advanced diploma courses are often chosen based on their performance in prior exams. The ultimate decision will be based on the merits of the candidates throughout the interview process.
  • Candidates interested in pursuing UG and PG degrees in fashion designing courses after 12th must take entrance tests such as NID, CEED, DAT, NIFT, and others, and will be chosen based on their results.

After having taken a brief look at the degree Fashion Designing courses after 12th, let us explore them a little in detail.

B.Des (Fashion Design)

This is the most sought-after course for those hoping to pursue a career in Fashion Designing after class 12. The 4-year integrated course consists of imparting professional and technical knowledge on different aspects of fashion, including fashion history, sewing, trends, and business communication. This course will give you an in-depth understanding of the history of fashion, fashion theory, current trends and forecasting, pattern making and draping, fashion sketching and Illustration, elements of colour & design, etc.

Fashion Designing course fees - The course is divided into 8 semesters, and the average fee per semester varies from INR 1,33,200 to INR 2,05,000 depending on the college.

NIFT (multiple locations), Pearl Academy – New Delhi, Maeer MIT Institute of Design – Pune, School of Fashion Technology (SOFT) - Pune, Symbiosis Institute of Design - Pune, etc.

B.Sc Fashion Design

It is a 3-year undergraduate Fashion Designing course. This course will help you to achieve technical skills, analyze and execute a design. The course structure includes topics like Fiber and Yarn Science, Pattern making, Garment construction, Apparel Production, grading, Analytical drawing, Leather designing, Fashion studies, Draping, Fashion forecasting, etc.

Fashion Designing course fees - The average fee for this course can range approx. from INR 1,60,000 - INR 2,50,000 per year.

Army Institute of Fashion & Design - Bengaluru, Indian Institute of Fashion Technology - Bengaluru, Vogue Institute of Art & Design - Bengaluru, PSG College of Arts & Science - Coimbatore, Government Home Science College - Chandigarh, etc.

B.A. (Hons.) Fashion Design

A 3-year Hons. degree in Fashion Design offers intensive coursework that will allow you to work more on research-based studies and design. It covers topics like colour mixing, elements of textiles, apparel construction methods, fashion illustration, surface development techniques, fashion studies, computer-aided design(CAD), fabric dyeing and printing, history of costumes, manufacturing technology, etc.

Fashion Designing course fees - The average fee for this course can range approx. from INR 1,17,000 - INR 2,25,000 per year.

Indian Institute of Art & Design - New Delhi, Singhania University - Rajasthan, ARCH Academy of Design - Jaipur, etc.

BBA Fashion Design & Management

A 3 year BBA course with a specialization in Fashion Design includes subjects like design, manufacturing and clothing distribution, wholesale and retail sales, as well as fashion media and photography. This course will allow you to gain in-depth knowledge of all the aspects of this sector. The study program has been designed in such a manner that it allows the students to understand various phases of Fashion designing from designing on paper to manufacturing as well as merchandising.

Fashion Designing course fees - The average fee for this course can range approx. INR 1,20,000 to INR 2,13,000 per year.

International college of Fashion- New Delhi, ARCH Academy of design - Jaipur, Arihant Group of Institutions - Bengaluru, etc.

B.FTech (Apparel Production)

This is a 4-year integrated course that will introduce you to the fundamentals governing aesthetic and design elements and management. It will also give you an insight into the machinery involved in the manufacturing of apparel including fabrics and trims, surface ornamentation, apparel design and development, manufacturing and allied areas.

The prerequisite qualification for admission into this course is 10+2 with in Physics, Chemistry & Maths.

Fashion Designing course fees - The average fee for this course can range approx. INR 1,50,000 - INR 2,50,000 per year.

Some top colleges - NIFT (Multiple locations), CET College of Science, Technology & Management - Kerala, Guwahati College of Architecture & Design - Guwahati, etc.
