Fine Arts

Fine Arts: Find out about fine arts as a career choice and how to pursue it.

Fine Arts are the study of different art forms such as drawing, sculpture, painting, literature, music, dance, architecture, and theatre. Fine Arts Courses also cover other fields such as Photography, Graphic Design, Interior Design, Drawing, etc. Aspirants seeking to pursue B.F.A must possess excellent creative, artistic, visualization, and presentation skills. If you complete your graduation with a BFA specialization, you will not only gain a formal education in your chosen field, but you will also learn creative skills.

Bachelor of Fine Arts, BFA in various specializations like Painting, Applied Arts, Master of Fine Arts, MFA, Diploma in Fine Arts, PhD in Fine Arts are fine arts courses after class 12th. Banaras Hindu University, Jamia Millia Islamia, Delhi University, JNU Delhi, Delhi College of Arts and Commerce, and Aligarh Muslim University are Top Fine Arts Colleges in India.

Students from any stream after class 12 can pursue bachelor's level fine arts courses, and masters in fine arts after graduation in arts or related disciplines. BHU UET, ITM NEST, JMI Entrance Exam, AMU Entrance Exam, and AUCET are some entrance exams for admission to top fine arts colleges. According to Glassdoor, the average salary offered in the domain of Fine Arts is around INR 7,35,690 per annum in India.

Career in Fine Arts

After completing Fine Arts Courses, one can either apply for jobs or go for higher studies in India as well as abroad. Here are some scopes post-completion of the courses in India and abroad.

  • After the completion of Fine Arts courses at the undergraduate level, candidates can opt for Postgraduate courses in Fine Arts and afterward for Ph.D. Courses in the same.
  • After completing PhD, one can become Art Professor or Teacher in Universities and Colleges.
  • Students can also study short-term Certification courses in Fine Arts to advance their skills and knowledge to keep themselves updated in the field of their interest.
  • Student can become Art Director, Photographer, Graphic Designer, Animator, Sculptor and Fine artist.

Fine Arts Course Details

  • Fine Arts courses are a study of the creation of diverse visual forms of art such as Photography, Drama, Painting, Sculptures, Architecture, Theatres, etc.
  • One can pursue Fine Arts courses at different levels of study such as Certification, UG, PG, Diploma, and Doctorate.
  • Some of the most sought-after Fine Arts courses are Certificate Course in Music, Certificate Course in Handloom Weaving and Handicraft, Diploma in Fine Arts, Bachelor of Fine Arts, etc.
  • The average fees of Fine Arts courses can range anywhere from INR 1,000 to INR 3,00,000 in India.
  • Top Colleges offering Fine Arts courses in India are Banasthali Vidyapeeth Jaipur, University of Lucknow, Amity University Lucknow, Tilak Maharashtra Vidyapeeth Pune, etc.

  • The College of Art at Delhi University offers Bachelor's and Master's Degrees in Fine Arts in a variety of specializations such as Applied Art, Painting, and Visual Communication.
  • Every year, the college offers a full-time four-year Bachelor of Fine Arts degree and a full-time two-year Master of Fine Arts degree to undergraduate students.
  • The candidate must have at least a 50% aggregate in the Senior School Certification Examination to be eligible. Students who have passed the Pre-University examination of an Indian board that is recognized by the University of Delhi are also eligible for admission to the BFA program
  • Candidates must have completed a Bachelor of Fine Arts Degree from a recognized university in the relevant specialization after 10+2 or an equivalent examination from a recognized board of education to be eligible for the MFA program.
  • Candidates with prior experience in their field of interest will be given preference. They must submit a proposal report on their preferred specialization, complete with visuals, slides, and other materials, indicating their intent to enrol in the course.

Eligibility Criteria for Fine Arts Courses

The academic eligibility requirements for pursuing Fine Arts Courses in India can differ from the one-course level or one university to the other. Below we have given a detailed tabular representation of Fine Arts Courses Eligibility Criteria at different course levels.

Fine Arts Courses Eligibility Criteria
Certificate Fine Arts Courses To be eligible for pursuing Certificate Fine Arts courses, the general eligibility requirement for students is to have at least passed their class 10th or class 12th from a recognized board in any stream (Arts, Science or Commerce).
Diploma Fine Arts Courses For Diploma courses in Fine Arts, a student must have passed their 10+2 with at least 50% marks in any stream from a recognized board
Undergraduate Fine Arts Courses For pursuing Undergraduate level courses in Fine Arts, a student must have passed their 10+2 with at least 50% marks in any stream with English and/or Hindi as core subjects from a recognized board.
Postgraduate Fine Arts Courses The eligibility requirements for pursuing Postgraduate level courses in Fine Arts is that a student must be a bachelor's degree holder in Fine Arts or BA from a recognized university with at least 50% to 60% marks.
Doctorate Fine Arts Courses The eligibility requirement for pursuing Doctorate level courses in Fine Arts is that a student must be a master's degree holder in Fine Arts from a recognized university with at least 55% marks.
