Hotel Management

What Is a Hotel Management? -Learn it in 5 Minutes

Hotel management is a detailed study of the hospitality and hotel industry. Greeting and meeting people on one’s responsibility is not rocket science but also it is not as easy as it seems. In hotel management colleges you will be imparted skills as to how you can manage at your best the operations of hotels, motels, resorts, etc. Hotel Management, a hospitality and travel specialization is gaining popularity among students who are planning to study a professional course immediately after completing their Class 12. What makes hotel management after class 12, one of the most sought-after courses is the wide array of opportunities available in the hospitality industry worldwide. Favourably, because it is one of the few professional courses that can be pursued by students from any stream in Class 12 (Arts, Science, and Commerce).

The Hotel Management course is intended to train students in different fields of the hospitality industry. Pursuing this course opens up a plethora of opportunities for students in top multinational companies catering to the food and beverage industry as well as in hotels, hospitals, airlines, and cruise lines.

Career & Scope

India is currently experiencing tremendous growth in the hospitality management sector, primarily because the country is being touted as one of the best international tourism destinations. The globalization of the tourism and hospitality industries has made a positive impact on the Indian economy, thereby creating great career opportunities in these industries.

Nowadays, people across the length and breadth of the world are keen to explore places in the national and international circuits. Even offbeat destinations are being chosen in the travel itinerary. This has initiated the establishment of accommodation and travel facilities in almost every corner of the nation.

The high influx of tourists combined with top-level MNC business meets, sporting events and international film festivals are spurring the need to create more tourism and accommodation facilities. This increase is directly proportional to job opportunities. After a degree/ diploma/ certificate course, one can get good jobs in innumerable hotels and restaurants in varied departments like Food and beverages, Accounting, Sales, Engineering/ Maintenance, Front Desk, and Security.

Besides these, there are plum career options in the sectors of:

  • Resorts and Guest Houses
  • Forest Lodges
  • Cruise Ships/ Shipping Companies
  • Airline Catering
  • Hospital Administration & Catering
  • Tourism Development Corporation/ Agencies
  • Hospitality Department in Indian Navy/ Armed Forces
  • Catering Department in Indian Railways
  • Hospitality Department in Corporate Houses
  • Kitchen Management in Schools/ Colleges/ Factories
  • Self-Business in the Role of an Entrepreneur

Apart from the conventional syllabus, the faculty takes the utmost care to develop the communication skills and interpersonal skills of a student. He or she is groomed to develop a polite demeanour that is a must for hospitality management jobs.

Subjects Taught in Hotel Management

Some common hotel management subjects that candidates are taught at the UG level include an introduction to food & beverage service, kitchen operations management, cookery, front office management, management of hospitality organizations, etc. On the other hand, hotel management subjects taught to aspirants in PG-level courses are principles and practices of management, hospitality operations and management, organizational behavior, food and beverage management, travel management, and the like.

Skill Requirements

Before applying to these courses, students need some basic skills that will guide them to success. Below are a few skills to consider:

  • Student must possess is management skills as they will require handling a lot of multiple-level personalities.
  • Should have patience and calmness in their nature.
  • Along with management skills the students need to have some interpersonal skills as they will be meeting customers and staff.
  • The students should have a supportive nature so that they can support their colleagues in the workplace.
  • The students need to possess a nature where they can encourage their teams at any situation.

Eligibility Criteria for Hotel Management Courses

The minimum qualification required to study Hotel Management courses in Class 12. The selection of an aspirant in government-approved hotel management colleges and institutes is done through entrance tests at the national level. One of the most popular entrance exams to secure admission to a hotel management course at the graduation level is the National Council for Hotel Management Joint Entrance Examination (NCHM JEE). NCHMCT JEE is conducted in April, every year.

The test comprises multiple-choice questions in English, Reasoning Capability, General Science, and General Knowledge. Candidates who successfully clear the entrance examination are called for personal interviews and group discussion rounds to judge the personal aptitude and personality of the candidate.

Private hotel management colleges select candidates based on PI and GD rounds. Some hotel management entrance exams that aspirants can consider appearing for to secure admission in the course of their choice are mentioned in the table below:

NCHMCT JEE MAH HM CET
UGAT Christ University Entrance Test
IHM Aurangabad Entrance Exam Oberoi STEP

Hotel Management Course Duration

The course duration of a degree course in hotel management is three years while diploma courses take two academic years to complete. The certificate courses in hotel management can vary between six months and one year.
