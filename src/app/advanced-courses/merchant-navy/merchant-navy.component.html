What is the Merchant Navy?

A merchant naval officer's job includes traveling across the world, handling cargo, inspecting ship direction, maintaining the ship, cleaning the boats, learning about different cultures, and so on. It is thrilling for people who are interested in traveling or have been bitten by the travel bug. They must make a huge sacrifice by not seeing their family for an extended period of time. Their work time is around 6-7 months, followed by a continuous holiday of 4-5 months. The best aspect of this position is that it is tax-free. Because you must dwell outside of India for at least six months, you will be granted NRI status.

Steps of How to join Merchant Navy in India Students who want to join the merchant navy must take the steps outlined below-

  • Step 1: Applicants must pass their class 12 board examinations with a minimum of 60% or equivalent CGPA and a requisite subject mix of Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Step 2: Students can then apply for and pass the JEE and IMU CET entrance tests, followed by an interview and medical examination stage.
  • Step 3: Applicants must then enroll in a BSc nautical science program at any of the country's leading nautical science schools.
  • Step 4: Students must then complete their 18-month cadet onboard training before applying for the Indian government's competence exam.
  • Step 5: Join as a Third Officer, then apply for rank upgrades and promotions. After beginning as a Third Officer, candidates can take yearly tests and pass them with flying colours.
